Javascript has 6 different types of values. These serve as multiple functions to the script. These are the 6 types:

  • Object- Not primitive. Anything that isn't a string, number, boolean, undefined, or null.
  • String- A collection of characters. Can be defined by using the "" or '' symbol.
  • Number- Used for calculations, can be integers or decimals. NaN means "not a number."
  • Boolean- They are ture or false statements, we use it for logic.
  • Undefined- They are non-values, they are given to variables with no assigned value.
  • Null- This is a place holder, if you were to put it in an equation. the null will be ignored.
