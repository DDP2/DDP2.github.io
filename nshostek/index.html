Biography

I designed this site in the way I did because it seemed like the simplest way to do it - the simplest, easiest method is most often the best option. The way the site is set up seems like the best way to meet all the requirements in a concise and quick manner. As far as the evolution of my learning goes, there isn't much to say. I didn't learn to think in a new way, as I did when exploring other programming languages. It was mostly memorization and generation of faux content. I don't care about a site having a color palette, I care about learning a new way of thinking about a problem. As far as features go, there isn't really anything to be proud of regarding the HTML and JS. The place on this site where my pride lies would have to be the text adventure I've included from last semester. I sank all but 2 weeks of the semester into that project, and while it is still incomplete, it is the focal point of my pride.
